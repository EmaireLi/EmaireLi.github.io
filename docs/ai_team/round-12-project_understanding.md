# Round 12 Project Understanding

## Current state

The site now has strong homepage routing, resilient/addressable archive discovery, accessible mobile recovery paths, project proof, curated First Reads, and an informed public guestbook entry. The main remaining visitor-flow gap is on direct article landings.

## Article evidence

- Five current posts end with a generic footer link `Back to archive`.
- Future post ownership spans `posts/new-post-template.html`, the editor renderer in `script.js`, and the XHS importer renderer.
- The archive destination is now complete without JavaScript and supports exact tag state with JavaScript.
- The corpus is only five posts and its topics are heterogeneous; semantic relatedness/personalization remains unsupported.
- Chronological adjacency is deterministic, but runtime-only continuation broadens shared-script, Unicode-path, edge, and fetch-failure risk.
- A static archive signature is progressive and lower risk but may add little beyond the existing footer.

## Round 12 constraints

- Research must explicitly allow a **no feature clears the bar** conclusion.
- If selected, implement exactly one visitor outcome and maintain five current shells plus all generator/template ownership points.
- Preserve the existing static archive escape route and article tone.
- Do not introduce relevance, personalization, AI, author availability, contact, or recruiting claims.
- Prefer ordinary semantic links and deterministic parity checks.
- Do not use the browser-policy limitation to overclaim visual QA.

## Question

Does a quiet static article archive signature, a single transparent chronological continuation, or another evidenced article-end improvement provide enough incremental visitor value to justify its maintenance surface? If not, recommend stopping the loop after documenting why.
